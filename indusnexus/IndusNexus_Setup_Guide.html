PHASE 4 · WEEKS 1–8

Legal & Compliance

UK companies have ongoing legal obligations from day one. The key registrations are HMRC (Corporation Tax + VAT), EORI (for importing/exporting), and Companies House annual filings. Staying compliant protects the company and avoids penalties.

01

HMRC — Register for Corporation Tax

Within 3 months of starting to trade (or generating income), Indus Nexus must register with HMRC for Corporation Tax. You'll receive a Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) by post within 10 working days. UK Corporation Tax rate: 25% on profits over £250K (19% for profits under £50K).

3 MONTH DEADLINE 25% rate (£250K+) 19% rate (<£50K)
📋 Register for Corporation Tax — GOV.UK
02

VAT Registration

Mandatory when taxable turnover exceeds £90,000/year (from April 2024). Voluntary registration before this threshold can be beneficial — you can reclaim VAT on business purchases (equipment, services). Standard UK VAT rate is 20%. For international B2B semiconductor sales, zero-rated VAT may apply.

MANDATORY at £90K turnover Voluntary before threshold 20% standard rate
📋 VAT Registration — GOV.UK
03

EORI Number — Essential for Import/Export

An Economic Operators Registration and Identification (EORI) number is required for all businesses importing goods into or exporting from the UK. This is critical for Indus Nexus semiconductor trading — you cannot clear goods through UK customs without it. Apply online, typically issued within 3 working days. It's free.

CRITICAL for semiconductor imports Free to apply 3 working days
🌍 Apply for EORI Number — GOV.UK
04

Annual Confirmation Statement & Accounts

Every year, Indus Nexus must file: (1) Confirmation Statement with Companies House — confirms company details are up to date (£34 online). (2) Annual Accounts with Companies House — abbreviated accounts for small companies. (3) Company Tax Return with HMRC — within 12 months of your accounting period end.

Confirmation statement £34/yr Annual filing deadline

Professional Indemnity & Business Insurance

For IT consultancy contracts (SIC 62020) and trading agreements, Professional Indemnity Insurance (PI) and Public Liability are typically required by enterprise clients before signing contracts. PI covers you against claims of financial loss caused by your professional advice or services. Cost: £500–£2,000/year for a small consultancy.

£500–£2,000/yr Required by enterprise clients

Export Controls — Dual-Use Goods

Some advanced semiconductor chips (especially AI accelerators, high-performance computing chips) may be subject to UK Export Control regulations and US export restrictions (EAR/ITAR). Before trading H100/H200 GPUs, Cortex A-series military-grade chips, or any items on the UK Strategic Export Control List, check and obtain necessary export licences.

NVIDIA H100/H200 — check restrictions UK Export Control Act 2002

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) — Know Your Customer

As a trading company, Indus Nexus must comply with UK Money Laundering Regulations 2017. Implement basic KYC (Know Your Customer) for all new business partners and clients. Maintain due diligence records for 5 years. Appoint a Nominated Officer (Money Laundering Reporting Officer — MLRO) if you handle significant financial flows.

FCA REGULATORY GUIDE

FCA Regulation — Do You Need a Licence?

This is the most frequently misunderstood aspect of starting a trading company in the UK. The answer depends entirely on what you trade and how you trade it.

✅

NO FCA Licence Required — Indus Nexus Phase 1

Physical semiconductor procurement, component distribution, and resale to businesses. IT consultancy and API integration services. Proprietary trading of your own company capital in listed equities (not for others). Import/export of electronic goods. Normal B2B commercial trading contracts.

⚠️

FCA Authorisation Required — Phase 2+ Activities

Managing or advising on investments on behalf of other people or businesses for reward. Running any kind of fund or pooled investment vehicle. Providing regulated investment advice to clients. Operating as a broker or dealer in financial instruments as a service to others. Any consumer credit activities.
