GROUND LEVEL TRADING · HOW TO ACTUALLY START

Import/Export Trading Operations

This is the practical ground-floor guide — the exact steps DK takes from day one to place Indus Nexus's first real semiconductor trade. No fluff, just action.

⚡

The Indus Nexus Trading Model — Simplified

Buy semiconductor components (chips, GPUs, MCUs, modules) from manufacturers and distributors in Asia (Taiwan, South Korea, China) and the US → ship to UK under your EORI → sell to UK/European/Middle East buyers at margin. Alternatively: source UK-available stock and export to South Asia (Pakistan, India, Bangladesh) where demand outstrips local availability.

01

Step 1 — Get Your EORI Number (Do This Today)

Without an EORI number, no shipment can clear UK customs. Apply online at GOV.UK — it's free and takes 3 working days. You'll use this number on every commercial invoice, packing list, and customs declaration for every trade Indus Nexus does.

URGENT — Day 1 TaskFree3 working days
🌍 Apply EORI — gov.uk/eori
02

Step 2 — Identify Your Commodity (HS/Tariff Code)

Every imported/exported product needs a Harmonized System (HS) code — a 10-digit number that determines the import duty rate and any restrictions. Semiconductors fall under Chapter 85. Use the UK Government's Trade Tariff tool to find the exact code for your product before placing any order.

8542 = Integrated Circuits8541 = Semiconductors8473 = Computer parts
📋 UK Trade Tariff Tool — trade-tariff.service.gov.uk
03

Step 3 — Source Your Suppliers

Start with authorised distributors (safest, no minimum order issues, quality guaranteed). Build toward direct manufacturer relationships as volume grows. The top UK-accessible distributor networks with corporate trade accounts available online:

Arrow Electronics ⭐ Avnet ⭐ Mouser Electronics RS Components Farnell Digi-Key
🔗 Arrow UK Corporate Account 🔗 Avnet Account Signup

Step 4 — Freight Forwarder & Customs Broker

A customs broker clears your goods through UK customs on your behalf — they file the import declarations, pay the duties, and handle HMRC paperwork. You pay them a fee (typically £50–£150 per shipment for standard goods). For regular trading, establish an account with a freight forwarder who handles door-to-door logistics AND customs clearance.

05

Step 5 — First Import: The Document Trail

Every import requires this exact set of documents. Indus Nexus must keep all of these for at least 4 years for HMRC audit purposes. Your customs broker generates the customs declaration (C88/E2) using your EORI.

✓ Commercial Invoice ✓ Packing List ✓ Bill of Lading / Air Waybill ✓ Certificate of Origin ✓ EORI Number ✓ HS/Commodity Code ✓ Import Duty Payment
📋 Import Goods UK Guide — GOV.UK
06

Step 6 — VAT on Imports (Postponed VAT Accounting)

When importing goods into the UK, import VAT is charged (currently 20%). However, if you're VAT registered, you can use Postponed VAT Accounting (PVA) — this means you don't pay VAT at the border. Instead, you declare it on your quarterly VAT return and claim it back in the same return. Net cost to you: £0. This is critical for cash flow — apply for PVA on your HMRC account.

PVA = Zero cash flow impactVAT registration essential
📋 Postponed VAT Accounting — GOV.UK
07

Step 7 — Selling / Exporting

When selling to overseas buyers (Middle East, South Asia, Europe), exports from the UK are zero-rated for VAT — you charge 0% VAT. You still need to declare the export to HMRC. For exports outside the UK, file an Export Customs Declaration. Payment: always use Letter of Credit (LC) or advance payment wire for new overseas buyers to protect against non-payment.

0% VAT on exportsExport declaration requiredUse LC for new buyers
📋 Export Goods Guide — GOV.UK
08

Step 8 — Getting Paid Internationally

For high-value semiconductor trades, standard bank transfers are fine for trusted partners. For new clients: request 30–50% upfront, balance on shipping document. Payment methods by region:

🇹🇼 Taiwan/Asia: SWIFT + LC 🇸🇦 Middle East: Advance TT wire 🇵🇰 Pakistan: Western Union/Wise 🇪🇺 Europe: SEPA/SWIFT

Use Wise Business for mid-market FX rates on international payments. Revolut Business for same-day payments to 160+ countries.
