# ESG & Sustainability Reporting

Authoritative lens over sustainability disclosure and assurance engagements. Covers preparer, reviewer, and auditor perspectives.

## When to use

- Preparer is scoping CSRD / ESRS, ISSB S1/S2, or first TCFD-aligned report.
- Double-materiality assessment needs review.
- Sustainability data pipeline needs controls.
- ISSA 5000 limited or reasonable assurance is being planned or executed.
- Regulator hot-spots (greenwashing, Scope 3, transition plan credibility).

## Frameworks in scope

| Framework | What it governs | Key artefacts |
|---|---|---|
| CSRD + ESRS E1-E5, S1-S4, G1 | EU-listed + large non-EU subsidiaries | Double-materiality, phased disclosures, XBRL tagging |
| ISSB IFRS S1 (general) + S2 (climate) | Global baseline, UK SDS from 2026 | Governance, strategy, risk, metrics & targets |
| TCFD | Climate scenario analysis | 4 pillars × 11 recommended disclosures |
| SASB | Industry-specific material topics | 77 industry standards |
| GRI | Impact reporting (outside-in) | Topic standards |
| SFDR | EU asset-manager product classification | Art 6 / 8 / 9 labels, PAI statements |
| UK TCFD / SDR | FCA-regulated firms | Entity + product disclosures, anti-greenwashing |
| ISSA 5000 | Assurance engagement standard | Limited vs reasonable assurance report |

## Preparer workflow

1. **Governance** — board oversight, ESG committee, accountability RACI.
2. **Double materiality** — impact (outside-in GRI) × financial (inside-out ISSB) matrix; stakeholder engagement evidence.
3. **Topic scoping** — map ESRS datapoints to business; apply phase-in reliefs.
4. **Data pipeline** — source systems → Scope 1/2/3 calc → consolidation → disclosure. Document provenance, emission factors, boundary.
5. **Controls** — SOX-equivalent controls over non-financial data; version control of emission factors.
6. **Narrative drafting** — transition plan, climate-resilient strategy, targets vs baseline, interim milestones.
7. **Tagging** — ESEF / ESRS XBRL tagging validation.
8. **Assurance readiness** — evidence file; management representations; materiality threshold for non-financial data.

## Assurance workflow (ISSA 5000)

1. **Engagement acceptance** — scope (limited vs reasonable), subject matter, criteria, intended users.
2. **Risk assessment** — inherent uncertainty, estimation risk (Scope 3), completeness risk.
3. **Materiality for non-financial data** — qualitative + quantitative benchmarks (e.g., 5% of total Scope 1+2 tCO2e).
4. **Evidence** — recalc emission factors, boundary tests, inclusion/exclusion testing, walkthrough of climate scenarios, management-expert evaluation (ISA 620 equivalent).
5. **Reporting** — conclusion format per limited (negative) vs reasonable (positive) assurance.

## Greenwashing red flags

- Unsubstantiated "net-zero by XXXX" without interim milestones or capex alignment.
- Scope 3 exclusions unexplained.
- Target-setting changed year-over-year without restatement.
- Product-level claims (e.g., "sustainable") without SFDR Art 9 substantiation.
- Emission factor updates buried in restatements.

## Deliverables

- Double-materiality matrix with stakeholder evidence log.
- ESRS datapoint inventory (mapped to source systems).
- Scope 1/2/3 calculation memo + boundary documentation.
- Transition plan narrative (capex, commitments, governance).
- ISSA 5000 assurance plan + evidence file + conclusion.
- Regulator response memo (FRC / FCA / EFRAG thematic reviews).

## Anti-patterns

- Bolting ESG onto finance close without separate reporting cycle / controls.
- One-off consultant-prepared baseline with no repeatable data pipeline.
- "Ambition" in narrative contradicted by capex plan in financials.
- Scope 3 screening that excludes material categories without rationale.

## Standards anchor

CSRD (Directive (EU) 2022/2464), ESRS Set 1 (Delegated Reg (EU) 2023/2772), IFRS S1/S2, TCFD final recommendations, GHG Protocol Corporate + Scope 3 standards, ISSA 5000, FRC Guidance on Climate-related Matters, FCA ESG Sourcebook, PRA SS3/19 + DP (Climate).
