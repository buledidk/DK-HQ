# Forensic & Fraud Investigation

Fraud risk response and investigation playbook for auditors, internal audit, compliance, and litigation teams.

## When to use

- ISA 240 fraud-risk response goes beyond standard audit work.
- Whistleblower allegation investigation.
- Financial crime / sanctions / AML breach investigation.
- Bribery & corruption allegation (UK Bribery Act, FCPA).
- Asset-tracing exercise.
- Expert witness report / deposition for dispute.
- Regulator-ordered investigation (s166 skilled person).

## Fraud typologies (ACFE)

| Category | Example schemes | Detection signature |
|---|---|---|
| Corruption | Bribery, conflicts of interest, illegal gratuities | Unusual vendor, round-number payments, off-market terms |
| Asset misappropriation | Skimming, larceny, false refunds, ghost employees, expense fraud | Segregation-of-duties bypass, voided transactions, vendor-employee bank match |
| Financial statement fraud | Revenue manipulation, expense concealment, reserve manipulation | Period-end JE reversals, round-number estimates, KAM pressure |

## Investigation methodology

1. **Triage** — validate allegation credibility, preserve evidence, legal hold, scope perimeter.
2. **Plan** — hypotheses, scope, team (forensic, IT, legal), budget, reporting cadence.
3. **Data preservation** — forensic images, email archive, chat logs, physical records; chain of custody log.
4. **Analytics** — targeted data tests (Benford's, duplicate payments, round-sum, out-of-hours JE, related-party).
5. **Interviews** — peripheral → central; witness statement format; right to counsel.
6. **Documentation** — every step evidenced, time-stamped, signed.
7. **Reporting** — factual, no conclusions of law, regulator-ready.
8. **Remediation** — controls redesign, dismissals, recoveries, referrals (NCA, FCA, SFO, DOJ).

## Red flags — master list

- JE posted by executives (bypassing normal approvers).
- JE posted outside business hours / weekends / period-end.
- Round-sum journals with vague descriptions.
- Vendor with same bank account as employee or recently changed bank account.
- Manual override of 3-way match.
- Duplicate invoices below approval threshold.
- Revenue in last week of quarter disproportionately higher.
- Reversal of accrual in Q+1 with no documentation.
- Related-party transactions with no independent review.
- Long-tenured employee refuses holidays.
- Lifestyle inconsistent with compensation (known to peers).

## ISA 240 response

When audit risk assessment flags fraud risk:

1. Assign senior team members / specialists.
2. Unpredictability in audit approach (vary locations, items tested).
3. Full-population JE testing with risk-based criteria.
4. Accounting estimates review for management bias.
5. Significant unusual transactions — business rationale evaluation.
6. Management override tests (ISA 240 ¶32).

## Interview framework (P.E.A.C.E.)

- **P**reparation & planning
- **E**ngage & explain
- **A**ccount, clarification & challenge
- **C**losure
- **E**valuation

Open questions first, funnel to specifics. Never lead. Document verbatim.

## Expert witness report standards

Under CPR 35 (UK) / Federal Rule 702 (US):
- Qualifications / CV appendix.
- Scope of engagement + materials reviewed.
- Assumptions and instructions.
- Methodology — reproducible.
- Findings — separated from opinion.
- Opinion — with reasoning.
- Duty to court statement.
- Signed declaration of truth.

## Financial crime — AML / sanctions

- **PEP + sanctions screening** — OFAC, OFSI, EU, UN; daily rescreening.
- **Transaction monitoring** — rule-based + anomaly detection; false-positive calibration.
- **Suspicious activity reports** — SAR filing (NCA UK / FinCEN US) with proper legal hold.
- **Sanctions breach** — self-report, voluntary disclosure, mitigating factors.

## UK Bribery Act / FCPA

- **UKBA s.7 offence** — failure by commercial org to prevent bribery; adequate-procedures defence requires risk assessment, top-level commitment, due diligence on associated persons, training, monitoring.
- **FCPA** — books-and-records + anti-bribery; strict liability for US-listed issuers; DPA / NPA mechanics.

## Deliverables

- Investigation plan + scoping memo.
- Evidence file (forensically imaged, hashed, chain-of-custody logged).
- Data analytics report (JE analytics, vendor matching, Benford's).
- Interview transcripts + witness statements.
- Final investigation report (facts, not legal conclusions).
- Expert witness report (CPR 35 / Rule 702 compliant).
- Remediation plan (control redesign + recoveries + referrals).

## Anti-patterns

- Tipping off suspect before evidence preservation.
- Mixing investigator role with legal advisor role (loses privilege).
- Reaching conclusions of law in investigation report.
- Weak chain of custody → evidence inadmissible.
- Scope creep → timeline slips, budget blown.
