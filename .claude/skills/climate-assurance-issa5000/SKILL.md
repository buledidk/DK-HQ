# climate-assurance-issa5000

Deep-dive for sustainability + climate assurance. ISSA 5000 (IAASB) approved Sep 2024, effective assurance engagements for periods beginning 15 Dec 2026 (earlier adoption encouraged). Complements `esg-sustainability-reporting` (authoring side) with the assurance lens.

## Standard landscape

| Standard | Focus | Role |
|---|---|---|
| ISSA 5000 | Overarching general requirements for sustainability assurance | New (effective 2026) |
| ISAE 3000 (revised) | Assurance other than audit + review of historical FI | Current default for sustainability |
| ISAE 3410 | Greenhouse gas statements | GHG-specific |
| ISRS 4400 | Agreed-upon procedures | Not assurance |
| Local standards | FRC standards (UK), SASB audit standards, etc. | Jurisdiction-specific |
| CSRD (EU) | Mandates assurance — limited from FY2024, reasonable TBC | Driver |
| California SB-261 + 253 | Climate disclosure + assurance | US state driver |

## Assurance engagement building blocks

### Three-party relationship
- **Assuror** (practitioner).
- **Responsible party** (management / TCWG of reporting entity).
- **Intended user** (investors, regulators, public).

### Subject matter + criteria
- **Subject matter**: the underlying information — emissions, policies, metrics, narratives.
- **Subject-matter information**: the reported output (statement, metric value, assertions).
- **Criteria**: benchmark to evaluate — ESRS, ISSB S1/S2, GHG Protocol, TCFD, SASB, entity-specific framework.

Criteria must be **suitable** — relevant, complete, reliable, neutral, understandable.

### Assurance continuum

- **Reasonable assurance** — high but not absolute; positive conclusion ("in our opinion, … is fairly stated").
- **Limited assurance** — lower; negative conclusion ("nothing has come to our attention …").
- Engagement risk scales inversely — limited assurance has higher engagement risk residual.

## Pre-engagement (ISSA 5000 + ISA 220 analogue)

- **Acceptance** — ethical requirements (IESBA International Code Part 5 — sustainability), independence, competence (scientific + subject-matter specialists), resourcing.
- **Engagement terms** — subject matter, criteria, level of assurance, report users, responsibilities, scope limitations, fees.
- **Scope definition** — reporting unit, metrics, narrative sections, operational boundary (financial control vs operational control vs equity share for GHG).
- **Materiality** — quantitative for metrics (often low threshold vs audit of FS — e.g. 5% of disclosed value for a climate metric); qualitative for narrative.
- **Double materiality** (CSRD) — impact materiality + financial materiality; practitioner assesses both for ESRS assurance.

## Planning

### Understanding the entity + sustainability reporting

- Business model + value chain + stakeholder analysis.
- Materiality assessment process (double materiality workshop, stakeholder engagement).
- Internal controls over sustainability reporting (ICoSR).
- Data sources — meters, invoices, ERP, carbon accounting software, third-party surveys.
- Estimation methods — emission factors (DEFRA, IEA, EPA), activity data × factor + proxies.
- Governance over ESRS process — sustainability committee, CFO-CSO integration.

### Risk assessment

- **Inherent risk factors** (scaled to sustainability):
  - Complexity (multi-site, Scope 3, value-chain).
  - Subjectivity (target setting, narrative disclosures, forward-looking statements).
  - Change (new metrics, restructuring, acquisitions).
  - Uncertainty (climate scenarios, emission factors).
  - Susceptibility to bias (greenwashing incentive).
- Identify significant risks — typically Scope 3, narrative targets, forward-looking, offsets, transition-plan credibility.

### Response design

- Controls reliance where ICoSR testable.
- Substantive procedures — recalculation, third-party confirmation (meter reading), site visits, observation, enquiry.
- Analytical procedures — year-on-year movement, intensity ratios, benchmark to sector peers.
- Specialist involvement (ISA 620 / ISSA 5000 equivalent) — climate scientists, engineers, actuaries.

## Evidence + procedures — sample

### Scope 1 (direct emissions)
- Fuel consumption records — invoice + meter.
- Vehicle fleet fuel use + emissions-factor application.
- Refrigerant leakage logs.
- Process emissions (cement, chemicals, steel).
- Site visits + walkthroughs.

### Scope 2 (purchased energy)
- Electricity + steam + heat + cooling purchase invoices.
- Location-based vs market-based methodology disclosure consistency.
- REC / GO (Renewable Energy Certificate / Guarantee of Origin) contract inspection.
- Residual-mix factor application.

### Scope 3 (value chain) — 15 categories
- Category-by-category analysis — is it material, is data reliable, is it included?
- Supplier surveys + response-rate analysis.
- Spend-based vs activity-based vs hybrid method — justify.
- Business travel, commuting, purchased goods + services, upstream transport, investments (Scope 3 Cat 15 — financed emissions under PCAF).
- Boundaries: financial-control vs operational-control vs equity-share; consistency across scopes.

### Narrative + forward-looking
- Target-setting evidence (SBTi validation, board approval).
- Transition-plan credibility — TCFD + FCA ESG standard.
- Interim milestones + accountability.
- Offsets policy + quality (Verra, Gold Standard, registry evidence, retirement).

### Double materiality (for CSRD / ESRS)
- Process documentation.
- Stakeholder input evidence.
- Impact materiality assessment + financial materiality assessment + combined matrix.
- Alignment with ESRS topical standards (E1 climate, E2 pollution, E3 water + marine, E4 biodiversity, E5 resources + circular economy, S1 own workforce, S2 workers in value chain, S3 affected communities, S4 consumers + end users, G1 business conduct).

## Group assurance considerations

- Similar to ISA 600R approach — components, component materiality, direction + supervision.
- Group-level consolidated metrics + component contributions.
- Acquisition + divestment treatment (restatement of baseline).
- Boundary changes disclosure.

## Reporting

### Limited assurance report
Structure (per ISAE 3000 + ISSA 5000 draft):
- Title + addressee.
- Scope + subject matter identification.
- Criteria.
- Inherent limitations.
- Responsibilities of management + practitioner.
- Summary of work performed.
- **Conclusion** — "based on the procedures performed + evidence obtained, nothing has come to our attention that causes us to believe that [subject matter] is not prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with [criteria]".

### Reasonable assurance report
- Similar structure but with positive opinion.
- "In our opinion, [subject matter] is prepared, in all material respects, in accordance with [criteria]".

### Modifications
- Qualified — limitation of scope or misstatement.
- Adverse — pervasive misstatement.
- Disclaimer — extreme limitation of scope.

## Common pitfalls + risks

- Scope 3 completeness — "we don't have data" isn't acceptable for material categories; use proxies + disclose method.
- Market-based Scope 2 without residual-mix + instrument-quality-criteria compliance.
- Offsets counted as "net zero" without vintage + additionality + double-counting checks.
- Forward-looking targets without a credible transition plan + interim milestones.
- Narrative boilerplate from previous year unchanged despite material changes.
- GHG baseline restatement threshold not consistently applied (typically 5% change).
- Metrics disclosed with different boundary across scopes (not comparable).
- Assurance scope carves out material elements without clear disclosure of what's covered vs excluded.

## Anti-patterns

- Treating limited assurance as "review" not assurance — ISSA 5000 is assurance engagement.
- Practitioner non-independence (consulting on ESG strategy + providing assurance).
- Relying on supplier self-reports for Scope 3 without sample verification.
- Emission-factor sources stale — applying 2015 DEFRA factors to 2024 activity data.
- Greenhouse-gas statement assurance without specialist involvement.
- Assurance report silent on forward-looking statements that are part of scope.
- Double-materiality process evidence limited to a single PPT — not audit trail.
