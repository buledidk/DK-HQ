# Transaction Services — Financial Due Diligence

Buy-side and sell-side financial DD, QoE analysis, and SPA mechanics for M&A transactions.

## When to use

- Buy-side or sell-side FDD on private targets.
- Quality of Earnings (QoE) for debt-financed transactions.
- Vendor due diligence (VDD) report preparation.
- SPA mechanics advisory (NWC peg, net debt, earn-out, locked box).
- Carve-out financial statement preparation.
- Red-flag DD in compressed timeline.

## Core DD workstreams

| Workstream | Key outputs |
|---|---|
| Quality of Earnings | Normalised EBITDA bridge, one-offs, run-rate adjustments, proforma LTM |
| Trading review | Revenue by customer/product/geography, volume/price/mix, churn, cohort retention |
| Working capital | Normalised NWC peg (12-month average, seasonality-adjusted), DSO/DIO/DPO trend |
| Net debt & debt-like | Debt, debt-like (pensions, leases, earn-outs, legal provisions), cash-like (restricted cash) |
| Cash flow | FCF bridge, capex (maintenance vs growth), working capital absorbers |
| Tax DD | Tax attributes, exposures, transfer pricing, NOLs, historic reorganisations |
| Forecast review | Management case vs sensitised case, assumption testing, milestone risk |
| Carve-out | Stand-alone adjustments, TSA costs, shared costs allocation |

## QoE methodology

1. **Reported EBITDA** (per management accounts / audited).
2. **Accounting adjustments** — policy alignment, cut-off, capitalisation vs expense, non-GAAP reconciliations.
3. **Non-recurring** — litigation settlements, restructuring, M&A fees, insurance recoveries, pandemic relief.
4. **Pro-forma** — annualised recent deals, run-rate cost savings (documented plan), new contract annualisation.
5. **Management adjustments** — scrutinise; reject speculative or un-evidenced.
6. **Adjusted EBITDA** → LTM → proforma LTM → run-rate.

Present as a **waterfall chart** with evidence tick-backs per adjustment.

## SPA mechanics — essential clauses

| Clause | Risk | Watch-out |
|---|---|---|
| Purchase price | Enterprise value vs equity value bridge | NWC + net debt definitions must be airtight |
| NWC peg | Normalised 12-month average | Seasonality; include/exclude items |
| Net debt | Reference date definition | Debt-like catch-all; cash sweep assumptions |
| Locked box | Leakage list | Permitted leakage; indemnity mechanics |
| Completion accounts | Adjustment mechanism | Accountant's determination clause |
| Earn-out | Contingent consideration | Measurable metric, anti-avoidance, cap/collar |
| Warranties & indemnities | Specific vs general | W&I insurance coverage; knowledge qualifiers |

## Red-flag DD (48-72 hour mandate)

Focused on deal-breakers only:
- Revenue recognition red flags (channel stuffing, bill-and-hold).
- Customer concentration (top 5 > 30%).
- Covenant breach risk.
- Tax exposures > 10% EV.
- Unresolved litigation > 5% EV.
- KPI definitions that don't hold up to audit.

## Carve-out preparation

1. **Perimeter definition** — assets, contracts, employees (TUPE/ARD), IP, customer contracts.
2. **Stand-alone costs** — identify shared costs (IT, finance, HR, legal, insurance) and model stand-alone basis.
3. **TSA costs** — transition services agreement duration + cost.
4. **Stand-alone EBITDA** = reported + carve adjustments (pro-forma).
5. **Historical financials** — apportionment methodology, SEC Rule 3-05 / IFRS equivalent.

## Deliverables

- DD report (executive summary, key findings, QoE, NWC, net debt, tax, cash flow, IT).
- QoE adjustment schedule (Excel, evidenced).
- NWC peg calculation (12-24 month view).
- Net debt + debt-like schedule.
- SPA mechanics advisory memo.
- Data book (supporting Excel pack).

## Anti-patterns

- Adding back everything as "one-off" → EBITDA loses credibility.
- NWC peg based on 3-month average → seasonality ambush at completion.
- Ignoring debt-like (leases, pensions, earn-outs) → equity value overstated.
- Management forecast taken at face value → no sensitised case.
- Carve-out stand-alone costs understated → post-deal P&L disappoints.

## Standards + references

Private company FS (IFRS / UK GAAP / US GAAP), ICAEW DD guidance, ABCA SPA standard forms, Rule 3-05 carve-out rules, IFRS 3 consolidation perimeter.
