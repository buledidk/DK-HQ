# Tax Provisioning — IAS 12 / ASC 740 / Pillar Two

Tax accounting and provisioning across IFRS, US GAAP, and OECD Pillar Two.

## When to use

- Year-end tax provisioning (current + deferred).
- DTA recoverability assessment (ISA 540 challenge).
- Uncertain tax positions evaluation under IFRIC 23.
- Pillar Two GloBE top-up tax calculation.
- Effective tax rate (ETR) reconciliation + narrative.
- Tax controls design (SOX 404 tax controls).
- Cross-border restructuring tax impact.

## IAS 12 core mechanics

| Component | Rule |
|---|---|
| Current tax | Tax payable on taxable profits for the period; measured at enacted/substantively enacted rates |
| Deferred tax | Temporary differences between carrying amount (IFRS) and tax base; measured at rates expected to apply when asset realised / liability settled |
| Initial recognition exception | No DT on initial recognition of asset/liability that is not a business combination, doesn't affect accounting or taxable profit (narrowed by 2021 IAS 12 amendment for leases + DT assets/liabilities arising from single transaction) |
| DTA recognition | Only to the extent probable that taxable profit will be available |
| Reassessment | Every reporting date |

## DTA recoverability — the ISA 540 hot spot

Management must evidence:
1. **Sources of future taxable profit**:
   - Future reversals of existing taxable temporary differences.
   - Projected future taxable profits.
   - Tax planning opportunities.
2. **Forecast period** — typically 3-5 years; beyond that requires strong justification.
3. **Convincing evidence** — required when there is a history of recent losses.
4. **DTA on tax losses** — recognition requires sufficient taxable temporary differences OR convincing other evidence.

Auditor challenges:
- Alignment of forecast with board-approved plan / budget.
- Consistency with going-concern assessment + goodwill impairment model.
- Historical accuracy of forecasts.
- Sensitivity of forecasts to reasonable alternative scenarios.
- Forecast uncertainty vs 3-5 year recognition horizon.

## IFRIC 23 — Uncertain Tax Positions

Two-step:
1. **Detection** — is it probable (>50%) tax authority will accept the treatment?
2. **Measurement** — if not probable, measure using either:
   - Most likely amount, OR
   - Expected value (probability-weighted).

Reassess each period with changes in facts/law.

**Evidence file**: tax opinions, counsel memos, advance tax rulings, rulings on similar fact patterns, audit history.

## Pillar Two — OECD GloBE (UK effective 2024, EU 2024)

Minimum 15% effective tax rate for MNE groups with revenue ≥ €750m:

| Top-up tax | Who pays | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **QDMTT** | Local jurisdiction | Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax — collected locally |
| **IIR** | Parent entity | Income Inclusion Rule — parent pays top-up on low-taxed subsidiaries |
| **UTPR** | Other group entities | Undertaxed Profits Rule — backstop if IIR not applied |

Key calculation:
1. GloBE income (starts from IFRS/US GAAP income with adjustments).
2. Covered taxes.
3. ETR per jurisdiction = Covered Taxes ÷ GloBE Income.
4. Top-up % = 15% − ETR.
5. Substance-based income exclusion (SBIE) — payroll + tangible asset carve-out.
6. Top-up tax = (Top-up % × Excess Profit) − QDMTT.

**Transitional CbCR safe harbour** (2024-2026): if jurisdiction meets de minimis / simplified ETR / routine profits test, presumed compliant.

**IFRS accounting** (IAS 12 amended May 2023): mandatory exception — do NOT recognise DT for Pillar Two; current tax only. Disclose impact qualitatively for future periods.

## ASC 740 — US GAAP differences vs IAS 12

| Topic | IAS 12 | ASC 740 |
|---|---|---|
| UTP threshold | Probable (>50%) | More-likely-than-not + measurement with >50% cumulative probability |
| Valuation allowance | DTA recognition threshold approach | Full DTA, then valuation allowance |
| Intraperiod allocation | Required | Different rules (especially OCI "backwards tracing") |
| Tax rate | Enacted or substantively enacted | Enacted only |
| Share-based payment | Through P&L with adjustments via equity | APIC pool / excess tax benefits through P&L since ASU 2016-09 |

## Effective tax rate reconciliation — audit essentials

Reconcile headline rate to actual ETR with each reconciling item:
- Non-deductible expenses (client entertainment, fines).
- Tax-exempt income.
- Prior-year adjustments.
- Changes in tax rates.
- Changes in recognised DTAs.
- Uncertain tax positions movement.
- Foreign rate differential.
- Pillar Two current-period impact.

Test: sum of reconciling items × 1 = actual − headline. If there's a "other" > 0.5%, drill down.

## Tax control framework (SOX 404)

Key controls:
- Tax accrual calculation review (2-eyes).
- Deferred tax proof (mapping carrying values to tax base).
- DTA recoverability sign-off (CFO + tax director).
- UTP evidence file (counsel opinions, external advice).
- Rate change impact calculation.
- Intercompany TP reconciliation.
- Pillar Two data feed validation.

## Deliverables

- Current tax calculation + reconciliation.
- Deferred tax roll-forward.
- DTA recoverability memo (forecast sources + evidence).
- UTP register + IFRIC 23 measurement.
- Pillar Two top-up tax calculation per jurisdiction.
- ETR reconciliation narrative.
- Tax disclosure draft (IAS 12 / ASC 740).
- Tax control matrix (SOX).

## Anti-patterns

- DTA recognition on >5-year forecast with no convincing evidence.
- Ignoring IFRIC 23 — still carrying provisions on "best estimate" basis pre-2019.
- Pillar Two treated as deferred tax (breaches the mandatory exception).
- ETR reconciliation aggregated in "other" > 1%.
- UTP positions not reassessed each period.

## Standards anchor

IAS 12 (inc. 2021 + 2023 amendments), IFRIC 23, OECD Pillar Two GloBE Model Rules + Commentary, UK Finance (No. 2) Act 2023 (Pillar Two), EU Pillar Two Directive 2022/2523, ASC 740, PCAOB AS 2501 + 2502 (tax estimates + UTP audit).
