# group-audit

ISA 600 (revised, effective Dec 2023) places the group auditor firmly in
charge of direction, supervision, and review.

## The ISA 600 (revised) shift

Post-revision: group auditor applies risk-based approach at group level;
designs planned procedures (incl. at components); provides direction +
supervision + review; holds responsibility for group-level evidence
sufficiency; enhanced two-way communication.

## Step 1 — Understand group structure

Legal entity hierarchy, ownership %, voting rights, business structure
(segments, geographies, shared services), consolidation method per entity
(full/equity/JV/NCI), group reporting package, IT environment (ERP?
consolidation tool — HFM, OneStream, Tagetik, Cognos?), intra-group
transactions, accounting-policy alignment, centralised vs decentralised.

## Step 2 — Identify components

Subsidiary (consolidated full), JV/Associate (equity), branch/division/
shared-service, consolidation-only (group-level journal, goodwill, tax),
head office.

## Step 3 — Scoping (risk-based)

For each component:
- Individually financially significant? (e.g. >15–20% of group)
- Contains RoMM significant to group? (may be small but high-risk)
- Aggregation effect of remaining components?

Categories:
| Category | Work |
|----------|------|
| Full-scope | ISA audit using component materiality |
| Specific-scope | Procedures on specific balances/transactions/disclosures |
| Analytical / review | Group-level analytics over residual |
| Out-of-scope | None — only if aggregate residual non-material |

Coverage: aggregate full + specific scope leaves residual not presenting RoMM.

## Step 4 — Component materiality

≤ Group materiality. Reflects component size + aggregation risk.
Component PM = x% of component materiality. Separate CTT at component level.
Reconcile vs statutory-audit materiality if component auditor using own.

## Step 5 — Instructions to component auditors

- Group EP identity + contact
- Scope (full/specific)
- Materiality (component, PM, CTT)
- Risk areas + required procedures (aligned to group risk assessment)
- Group accounting policies
- Reporting timetable + format (clearance memos, SUM, WP extracts, reps)
- Ethics + independence
- Confidentiality
- Access rights for group-auditor inspection
- Escalation protocol
- Communication checkpoints

## Step 6 — Direction + supervision + review

- Planning meeting
- Risk-assessment sign-off by group team
- Interim / pre-year-end touchpoint
- Completion meeting — review of working papers (risk-based), misstatements,
  significant matters, KAMs
- Final clearance

Significant components: group-level file review expected.

## Step 7 — Evaluate component auditor's work

Competence + independence + capability; sufficiency + appropriateness of
evidence; identified risks addressed; scope limitations?

Insufficient evidence? → further work, group-team procedures, or opinion impact.

## Step 8 — Group-level consolidation procedures

- Consolidation mechanics (full / proportionate / equity / JV)
- Elimination of intra-group (P&L + BS; unrealised inventory profit;
  intra-group loans + interest; dividends)
- FX translation (IAS 21): monetary/non-monetary for branches; closing-rate
  for foreign operations; CTD/OCI
- Goodwill by CGU; impairment at group (IAS 36)
- NCI: presentation + share of CI; transactions changing NCI %
- Acquisitions / disposals (IFRS 3 / IFRS 10): PPA, re-measurement, gain/loss
- Group-level estimates: impairment, DTA, tax, restructuring
- Related-party: intra-group + with controlling shareholders / PSCs

## Step 9 — Completion

- Aggregate misstatements across components
- Evaluate vs group materiality
- Obtain group-level written representations
- KAMs at group level (ISA 701)
- EQR for listed / PIE / high-risk groups
- Subsequent events at group + significant-component level

## Step 10 — Reporting

Single auditor's report on group FS. Component auditor reports stand for
statutory purposes, not referenced in group report.

## Automation

- Scoping matrix from TB + risk flags; coverage % auto-calc
- Materiality auto-cascade from group to component
- Template instruction letter per scope
- Component-status dashboard (planning → fieldwork → review → clear)
- Intra-group matching reports; FX application; elimination proofs
- Automated SUM schedule across components
- Risk-based sampling of component papers for review
- Structured two-way portal with audit trail

## Anti-patterns

- Delegating significant-risk procedures entirely to component
- Component materiality = group materiality (over-scoping)
- Skipping consolidation-mechanics audit because "system does it"
- Evaluating component work on summary schedules only
- No in-scope coverage of disposal gain/loss
- PPA step-ups accepted without valuation specialist
