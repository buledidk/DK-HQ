# auditors-report

Every word scrutinised by regulators, investors, and plaintiff bar.

## Opinion decision tree

```
1. Evidence sufficient + appropriate?
   NO → scope limit:
        Material + pervasive → Disclaimer (ISA 705)
        Material, not pervasive → Qualified "except for..."
   YES ↓
2. FS free from material misstatement?
   NO → misstatement:
        Material + pervasive → Adverse (ISA 705)
        Material, not pervasive → Qualified "except for..."
   YES ↓
3. Going concern (ISA 570)
   Inappropriate basis → Adverse
   MU disclosed → Unmodified + "Material Uncertainty Related to GC" section
   MU not adequately disclosed → Qualified or Adverse
4. Otherwise → Unmodified
```

## Report structure (ISA 700 revised 2016, listed, UK)

1. Title: "Independent auditor's report to the members of [Entity]"
2. Opinion (first)
3. Basis for opinion (ISAs UK + FRC Ethical Standard; independence)
4. Conclusions relating to going concern (ISA 570 ¶22 — UK moved higher)
5. Key Audit Matters (ISA 701, listed)
6. Other Information (ISA 720)
7. Opinion on other matters prescribed by CA 2006 (adequate records,
   director remuneration, strategic report)
8. Matters required to report by exception
9. Responsibilities of directors
10. Auditor's responsibilities
11. Use of our report
12. Engagement partner; firm; address; date

## Key Audit Matters (ISA 701)

Required for listed (UK by law; FRC policy for others voluntarily).

Selection: matters communicated to TCWG, of most significance. Consider:
- Higher assessed RoMM or significant risk
- Significant auditor judgments (inc. estimation uncertainty)
- Effect of significant events/transactions

Per KAM:
1. Why it was of most significance (specific, not generic)
2. How we addressed it (specific procedures, not generic list)
3. Reference to FS disclosures (note number)
4. Outcome language — UK often includes "Our observations" paragraph

Pitfalls: boilerplate; kitchen-sink procedure lists; vague outcomes;
disconnect from risk register.

## Modifications (ISA 705)

| Circumstance | Material not pervasive | Material + pervasive |
|--------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| FS materially misstated | Qualified | Adverse |
| Unable to obtain sufficient evidence | Qualified | Disclaimer |

Pervasive: not confined to specific elements; if confined, substantial
proportion; fundamental to users' understanding.

### Wording templates

Qualified (misstatement):
> "...except for the effects of the matter described in the Basis for
> Qualified Opinion section, the FS give a true and fair view..."

Qualified (scope limit):
> "...except for the possible effects of the matter described..."

Adverse:
> "...because of the significance of the matter..., the FS do not give
> a true and fair view..."

Disclaimer:
> "We do not express an opinion... we have not been able to obtain
> sufficient appropriate audit evidence..."

## Emphasis of Matter + Other Matter (ISA 706)

**EoM** — draw attention to a matter presented or disclosed in FS that is
fundamental to users. Does NOT modify opinion. Placed after Basis (+ KAMs).
Typical: prior-period restatement, major uncertainty not reaching MU-GC.

**OM** — matter other than presented/disclosed. Used sparingly. Typical:
regulatory-purpose reports; PY FS audited by predecessor; appointment
circumstances.

## Other Information (ISA 720)

- Read annual report
- Consider material inconsistency between OI and FS / auditor knowledge
- Report if so; report material misstatements of fact in OI
- UK: include OI statement in report

## Going-concern paragraph (ISA 570 as revised)

- Unmodified + MU disclosed → "Material Uncertainty Related to Going Concern"
  section, referencing note
- Unmodified + no MU but GC is key matter → may be KAM
- Adverse → GC basis inappropriate wording

## UK opinions on other matters (CA 2006)

- Strategic + Directors' Report: in accordance with law; consistent with FS
- Directors' remuneration (listed, audited part)
- Adequate accounting records (s.498)
- Returns adequate (s.498)
- Information + explanations received (s.498)
- Director remuneration disclosures per law

## Sign-off + dating

- Report date = date of sufficient appropriate evidence
- Must be ≥ directors' approval date
- Subsequent events to report date (ISA 560 ¶6)
- Dual dating if subsequent event triggers re-opened FS (ISA 560 ¶12)

## Anti-patterns

- Opinion wording changed without firm-review
- KAMs pulled from last year
- EoM used where disclosure clarity is the real issue
- Disclaimer on one area used to avoid proper scope discussion
- Report date before last subsequent-event procedure
