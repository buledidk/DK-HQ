# Treasury & Hedge Accounting

IFRS 9 hedge accounting, IRRBB management, and treasury policy — for corporates and banks.

## When to use

- Applying hedge accounting for the first time or re-designating under IFRS 9.
- Preparing / reviewing hedge documentation (prospective + retrospective).
- Effectiveness testing (quantitative + qualitative).
- IRRBB management + regulatory reporting (banks).
- Hedging strategy design (FX, IR, commodity, equity).
- Treasury policy drafting / review.

## IFRS 9 hedge accounting model

Three hedge types:

| Type | What it hedges | Accounting |
|---|---|---|
| Fair value hedge | FV changes in recognised asset/liability or firm commitment | Gain/loss on hedging instrument in P&L; offsetting adjustment to hedged item in P&L |
| Cash flow hedge | Variability of cash flows from recognised asset/liability or highly probable forecast transaction | Effective portion in OCI (CFH reserve); reclassified to P&L when hedged item affects P&L |
| Net investment hedge | FX translation of net investment in foreign operation | Effective portion in OCI (FCTR); reclassified to P&L on disposal |

## Mandatory hedge documentation (IFRS 9 §6.4.1)

At inception — must be formal and written:

1. **Risk management objective and strategy** (per risk type).
2. **Hedged item** — specific asset, liability, firm commitment, forecast transaction, component of risk.
3. **Hedging instrument** — full description (ISIN, notional, tenor, counterparty).
4. **Nature of risk being hedged** (benchmark rate, FX rate, commodity price).
5. **Hedge ratio** — quantity of hedging instrument ÷ quantity of hedged item.
6. **Sources of ineffectiveness** anticipated.
7. **Method for assessing effectiveness** (qualitative or quantitative; frequency).
8. **Economic relationship** — between hedged item and hedging instrument.
9. **Credit risk analysis** — does it dominate value changes?

## Effectiveness testing

IFRS 9 moved to a **prospective only** criteria-based test (qualitative allowed):
- Economic relationship exists (values move in opposite directions).
- Credit risk does not dominate.
- Hedge ratio is consistent with risk management.

Quantitative methods (when qualitative is insufficient):
- Dollar-offset (cumulative or period).
- Regression analysis (R², slope, intercept; 80%-125% rule NOT IFRS 9 but still common).
- Hypothetical derivative method.
- Volatility reduction measure.

**Rebalancing** required (not de-designation) if hedge ratio drifts; document reason + new ratio.

## Sources of ineffectiveness — common list

- Counterparty credit risk.
- Basis differences (hedging benchmark vs underlying).
- Timing mismatches.
- Notional mismatches.
- Tenor mismatches.
- Forward point separation.
- Currency basis in cross-currency swap (can be cost-of-hedging under IFRS 9).

## Cost of hedging (IFRS 9 innovation)

Three separable elements can be excluded from hedge and deferred in OCI as "cost of hedging":
- Forward element of forward contracts.
- FX basis spread (in cross-currency swaps).
- Time value of options.

Amortised to P&L systematically.

## IRRBB (banks)

Interest rate risk in the banking book — PRA SS31/15, EBA GL/2022/14:

- **Economic Value of Equity (EVE)** — PV change under 6 prescribed scenarios.
- **Net Interest Income (NII)** — 12-month projected change under rate shocks.
- **Behavioural modelling** — NMD (non-maturing deposits), prepayments, pipeline.
- **Outlier test** — EVE decline > 15% of Tier 1 → regulatory flag.
- **Disclosure** — Pillar 3; IFRS 7 sensitivity.

## Treasury policy — essential sections

1. Governance (ALCO, treasury committee, board delegation).
2. Risk appetite (FX, IR, credit, liquidity, counterparty, commodity).
3. Permitted instruments + ISDA / CSA requirements.
4. Counterparty limits + credit thresholds.
5. Hedging guidelines (minimum, maximum, tenor).
6. Investment policy (credit quality, duration, concentration).
7. Funding & liquidity (LCR, NSFR for banks; minimum cash for corporates).
8. Reporting & breaches (frequency, escalation, board notification).

## Deliverables

- Hedge documentation pack (per relationship).
- Effectiveness testing file + methodology memo.
- Journal entries for inception, re-measurement, ineffectiveness, discontinuation.
- IFRS 7 + IFRS 9 disclosures draft.
- IRRBB report (EVE, NII, behavioural assumptions, outlier test).
- Treasury policy (board-approved version).

## Anti-patterns

- Hedge documentation drafted after trade → fails IFRS 9 §6.4.1.
- Effectiveness testing treated as annual → should be on designation + each reporting date.
- Ignoring credit risk — if CVA/DVA dominates, hedge fails.
- Using "hedge" term for economic hedges without IFRS 9 designation → no accounting relief.
- Net investment hedge designated for investment in subsidiary's functional currency (not foreign operation).

## Standards anchor

IFRS 9 §6 (hedging), IFRS 7 (disclosure), IAS 21 (FX), IAS 32 + IFRS 9 (classification), PRA SS31/15 + EBA GL/2022/14 (IRRBB), ISDA documentation standards.
