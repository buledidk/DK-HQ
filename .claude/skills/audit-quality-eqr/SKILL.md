# audit-quality-eqr

Aligned with ISQM 1, ISQM 2, ISA 220 (revised), FRC AQR / PCAOB / IAASB.

## ISQM 1 — firm system of quality management

8 components:
1. Governance + leadership
2. Relevant ethical requirements (incl. independence)
3. Acceptance + continuance
4. Engagement performance (direction, supervision, review, consultation,
   EQR, documentation)
5. Resources (human, tech, intellectual, financial)
6. Information + communication
7. Monitoring + remediation
8. Risk-assessment process

Cycle: Quality objectives → Risks → Responses → Monitoring → Remediation →
Evaluation (annual, by Managing Partner equivalent).

## ISQM 2 — Engagement Quality Review (EQR)

Required for: listed entities; other audits per ISQM 1 risk assessment;
review/other assurance where firm policy requires.

### Nature
Objective evaluation, BEFORE report dated, of: significant judgments;
conclusions; FS + draft auditor's report.

### Reviewer qualifications
- Competent (qualifications + recent partner-level experience in industry)
- Authority (able to challenge EP)
- Objective (not involved in engagement; 2–3 years cooling-off if prior)
- Sufficient time

### Scope minimum
- Discussion of significant matters with EP
- Review of FS + auditor's report
- Review of selected documentation re significant judgments + conclusions
- Evaluation of independence, judgments, significant matters to TCWG

### Documentation
- Procedures performed
- Date of completion
- Matters considered
- Statement that EQR raised no matters preventing EP opinion
- Unresolved matters (if any)

**Report cannot be dated before EQR complete + cleared.**

## ISA 220 (revised) — engagement-level quality

Applies every engagement. EP responsibilities:
- Overall responsibility for quality
- Ensure team (incl. specialists + component auditors) competent + capable
- Direction, supervision, review appropriate to risk + team experience
- Consultation on difficult/contentious matters; documented
- Differences of opinion resolved + documented
- Engagement documentation assembly + archive

## Root-cause analysis (RCA)

Finding triggers RCA. Steps:
1. Facts → what happened
2. Causal chain → 5-Whys
3. Categorise: people / process / tools / governance / external
4. Pattern check — happened before? similar engagements?
5. Remediation: immediate (engagement) + systemic (policy/tool/training/governance)
6. Effectiveness monitoring — verify remediation works

## FRC AQR common findings (themes)

- GC procedures insufficient
- Impairment sensitivity / challenge weak
- IFRS 15 complexity not fully addressed
- L3 FV — insufficient challenge to unobservable inputs
- Group audits — group-team direction insufficient
- Fraud risk — JE testing too narrow
- Estimates — no auditor range, no bias challenge
- Skepticism — evidence of challenge + follow-through weak

## Firm-level cultural measures

- Time-budget pressure acknowledged + managed
- Partner KPIs beyond billings (quality, EQR findings, retention)
- Mandatory partner rotation (5-yr listed, FRC Ethical Standard)
- Tone-at-top visible (example decisions, remuneration tied to quality)

## Automation for quality

- Inspection-findings knowledge base (searchable by topic + standard)
- Engagements-at-risk dashboard (overruns, EP tenure, complex estimates,
  regulator watch-list)
- EQR selection tool: complexity + risk scoring for non-listed EQRs
- Auto-check mandatory procedures (JE testing, reps, GC)
- Consultation-log completeness
- Archive closure at 60 days with reminders

## Anti-patterns

- EQR done after report signed (rubber-stamp)
- EQR reviewer too close to engagement
- RCA stopped at "team should have done more"
- Remediation = more training; no process/tool change
- Quality metrics not reported to firm TCWG
- Monitoring findings not actioned before next cycle
