# Governance Structure Enhancement

Design and review of governance frameworks for boards, executive committees, and regulated subsidiaries. UK + global, with heavy FS regulatory lens.

## When to use

- New entity / post-IPO / post-M&A governance design.
- Board / committee effectiveness review.
- Regulator pre-visit / s166 / SYSC thematic.
- Delegated-authority rewrite.
- SMCR / SMF mapping.
- Pre-IPO governance readiness.
- Stewardship Code reporting.

## Governance layers — what you are designing

```
Shareholders (Stewardship Code, AGM)
   │
   ▼
Board of Directors
   │
   ├─ Audit Committee          (ISA 260, UKCGC, FRC Guidance)
   ├─ Risk Committee           (FS only; required for banks/insurers)
   ├─ Remuneration Committee   (UKCGC F, PRA Rem Code, CRD Art 94)
   ├─ Nomination Committee     (UKCGC D)
   ├─ ESG / Sustainability Cmte (optional; CSRD-driven)
   └─ Technology / Ops Cmte    (optional; regulated firms)
   │
   ▼
Executive Committee (CEO + direct reports)
   │
   ├─ ALCO (banks / insurers)
   ├─ Credit Committee (banks)
   ├─ Investment Committee (asset managers)
   ├─ Model Risk Committee
   ├─ New Product / Material Change Committee
   ├─ Operational Risk Committee
   └─ Disclosure Committee (listed)
   │
   ▼
Management layer (functions, 1st line)
```

Design decisions: how many committees, composition, chairs, delegation, reporting lines, frequency, quorum, conflicts.

## UK Corporate Governance Code 2024 — key provisions

| Section | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **A. Board leadership + company purpose** | Clear purpose, strategy, culture, workforce engagement mechanism |
| **B. Division of responsibility** | CEO ≠ Chair; SID; ≥ half board independent NEDs; chair independent on appointment |
| **C. Composition, succession, evaluation** | Skills matrix; annual board evaluation; external evaluation every 3 years; diversity (listing rule 9.8.6R) |
| **D. Audit, risk + internal control** | Internal control attestation (new 2026: declaration of effectiveness); going-concern + viability; whistleblowing; AC terms of reference |
| **E. Remuneration** | Executive pay linked to long-term success; clawback / malus; NED fees reasonable |

**The 2026 internal-control attestation** is the governance hot-button: boards must publicly declare their material controls operated effectively for the period (or explain why not).

## Wates Principles (large private companies)

Alternative for private companies > 2 of: 2k employees / £200m T/O / £2bn BS:
1. Purpose & leadership
2. Board composition
3. Director responsibilities
4. Opportunity & risk
5. Remuneration
6. Stakeholder relationships & engagement

Disclose on a "apply and explain" basis in strategic report / directors' report.

## UK Financial Services — SMCR

Senior Managers Regime structure:

| Layer | Who | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **SMFs** (Senior Management Functions) | CEO, CFO, CRO, Chair, SID, Chair of committees, Head of IA, etc. | FCA/PRA approval, Statement of Responsibilities, Duty of Responsibility |
| **Certification Regime** | Significant-harm roles below SMFs | Firm certifies fit & proper annually |
| **Conduct Rules** | All staff | Individual rules + senior-manager rules |
| **MRM** (Management Responsibilities Map) | Single document | End-to-end accountability mapped to SMFs |

MRM is the skeleton of FS governance — update on every SMF change.

## Board composition design — the skills matrix

Rows = directors. Columns = skills / experience:

- Accounting / finance / audit (≥ 1 recent & relevant)
- Industry / sector
- Risk management
- Technology / cyber / data
- Climate / ESG
- International / geography
- Legal / regulatory
- Commercial / go-to-market
- HR / remuneration
- M&A / capital markets

Gaps → recruitment brief. Overlaps → succession / rotation plan.

**Independence** — FRC 10 tests (former employee, cross-directorship, material business relationship, etc.). Chair independent on appointment.

**Tenure** — typically 9 years max for NEDs; chair usually 9 from appointment (including NED time).

**Diversity** — Listing Rule 9.8.6R (women on board ≥ 40%; ≥ 1 of Chair/CEO/CFO/SID woman; ≥ 1 director from ethnic-minority background). Parker Review targets.

## Committee design essentials

### Audit Committee
- ≥ 3 independent NEDs.
- Chair with recent & relevant financial experience.
- ToR: external audit, internal audit, financial reporting, internal controls (incl. 2026 attestation), whistleblowing.
- Private session with external + internal auditors at every meeting.

### Risk Committee (FS required)
- Independent NEDs majority.
- Reviews risk appetite, ICAAP/ILAAP, RRP, significant risk events.
- Private session with CRO.

### Remuneration Committee
- ≥ 3 independent NEDs.
- Pay ratios (s439A UK CA 2006 quoted) disclosed.
- Malus / clawback triggers tested.

### Nomination Committee
- Chair-led (sometimes SID for chair succession).
- Succession plan (emergency + medium-term).
- Skills-matrix gap analysis.

## Delegated Authorities — design principles

- **Reserved matters** (board only): strategy, budget, material M&A, capital structure, dividend policy, appointments of SMFs, material litigation > threshold, entering new jurisdictions, approving policies (risk appetite, conflicts, whistleblowing, anti-bribery, sanctions).
- **Delegated to CEO / ExCo**: operational, within risk appetite, within budget.
- **Sub-delegated**: operational committees (Credit, ALCO, IC).
- **Tiered limits**: £ + risk + counterparty thresholds.
- **Escalation triggers**: material adverse, covenant breach, regulatory notification, cyber incident.

Review DoA annually + on any org change.

## Board effectiveness review

| Review type | Cadence | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Self-assessment | Annual | Action plan |
| Internal facilitator | Alternative / supplementary | Themes + interventions |
| External | Every 3 years (UKCGC) | Recommendations + action plan + published summary |

Topics covered: board dynamics, decision quality, information quality, time on strategy vs operations, committee effectiveness, succession, chair effectiveness, NED contribution.

Tool options: questionnaires, 1:1 interviews, meeting observation, document review, peer benchmarking.

## Governance maturity model (5 levels)

| Level | Characteristics |
|---|---|
| 1. Ad hoc | Decisions reactive, minutes thin, committees overlap |
| 2. Basic | Structure in place, minutes documented, terms of reference drafted |
| 3. Defined | Clear delegations, skills matrix maintained, cycle forward planner, internal review |
| 4. Managed | KPI-driven, external evaluation, integrated reporting, stakeholder-responsive |
| 5. Optimising | Anticipatory (horizon scanning), culture + conduct led, stewardship leader |

Diagnose current level → plan to next level in 12-18 months (don't skip levels).

## Stakeholder + stewardship narrative

- **s.172 statement** (UK CA 2006) — how directors considered stakeholders; embed in strategic report.
- **Stewardship Code** (FRC 2020) — if investor; apply/explain 12 principles.
- **Dear CEO / s166** responses — board-level tone, remediation plan, governance uplift.
- **Regulator fireside** — board members must articulate risk appetite, top risks, culture — not read from script.

## Deliverables

- Board charter + terms of reference (each committee).
- Skills matrix + gap analysis.
- Management Responsibilities Map (FS).
- Delegated Authority matrix.
- Decision rights / RACI.
- Governance forward planner (12-month meeting cadence + agenda themes).
- Board effectiveness review report + action plan.
- Governance statement in Annual Report (UKCGC / Wates).
- Regulator-facing governance pack (SYSC / s166 / thematic).

## Anti-patterns

- Committees too many → directors spread thin, duplication.
- Committees too few → AC or board clogged with detail.
- ToR not refreshed since listing → stale responsibilities.
- Chair dual-hat (executive responsibilities) → independence breached.
- CEO chairs their own committee → conflict.
- Board pack too long (> 300 pages) with no executive summary.
- Strategy item always last on agenda → always rushed.
- Minutes record what was said, not what was decided.
