# sector-public-charity

Sector expertise for UK public-sector + not-for-profit engagements.

## Entity types + reporting framework

| Entity | Framework | Regulator / Oversight |
|---|---|---|
| Charity (large) | Charities SORP (FRS 102) | Charity Commission (E&W), OSCR, CCNI |
| Charity (micro) | Receipts + payments or SORP FRS 105 | Charity Commission |
| Academy / MAT | Academies Accounts Direction (AAD) | DfE, ESFA, Academy Trust Handbook |
| Higher Education | HE/FE SORP (FRS 102) + FE/HE DfE regs | OfS, SFC, HEFCW |
| Further Education | FE SORP | ESFA, DfE |
| NHS Trust / FT / ICB | NHS GAM (IFRS with adaptations) | NHS England, DHSC, Monitor legacy rules |
| Central govt dept + ALBs | HM Treasury FReM | Cabinet Office, HMT |
| Local authority | CIPFA / LASAAC Code (IFRS-based) | DLUHC, CIPFA, PSAA |
| Registered Social Landlord | Housing SORP (FRS 102) | Regulator of Social Housing |

## Charities (SORP FRS 102)

- SoFA (Statement of Financial Activities) replaces P&L — split by fund class (unrestricted, restricted, endowment) + incoming / expenditure / gains+losses.
- Trustees' annual report — required content: public benefit, strategic aims, achievements, financial review, reserves policy, risk review, structure + governance, plans for future.
- Reserves policy — target free reserves + rationale.
- Income recognition — entitlement + probability + measurement.
- Related parties (s33 FRS 102) — lower threshold; trustees + connected persons.
- Grant income — donor-restricted vs unrestricted; performance-related conditions.
- Heritage assets — IAS 16 + SORP specifics; disclosure of significance + holdings.
- Permanent endowment — preservation of capital + distribution limit (or total-return election).

Audit / independent examination threshold — income >£1m (audit) OR >£250k + assets >£3.26m; otherwise independent examination.

## Academy trusts + MATs

- Academies Accounts Direction (AAD) — annual update; financial year 1 Sep – 31 Aug.
- Format prescribed — additional notes beyond FRS 102 SORP: related-party disclosures, accounting officer statement (regularity), governance statement, trustees' report, Reserves policy.
- **Regularity audit** — parallel assurance opinion on funds used for intended purposes per DfE funding agreements. Reporting Accountant reports to ESFA.
- LGPS (Local Government Pension Scheme) — TPS and LGPS multi-employer DB; IAS 19 / FRS 102 s28 actuarial valuation via scheme actuary.
- Related-party transactions — SECONDARY disclosure threshold £5k (tighter than SORP); trustee / connected party transactions.
- ESFA spot-checks + Financial Management Good Estate Management Standards.

## Local authorities

- CIPFA / LASAAC Code — IFRS-based with statutory overrides.
- Statutory vs accounting: capital receipts, MRP, HRA ring-fence, statutory pension reserve, accumulated absences.
- Collection Fund — separate statement (council tax + NDR).
- Heritage assets, community assets, infrastructure, investment property — different bases.
- Housing Revenue Account (HRA) — ring-fenced.
- LGPS — IAS 19 liability with explanatory reserve adjustment.
- Group accounts if LA controls subsidiaries / has JVs / associates.
- **Code of Audit Practice** (NAO + PSAA) — Auditor's opinion + Value-for-Money conclusion on 3 reporting criteria (financial sustainability, governance, economy/efficiency/effectiveness).

## NHS (GAM)

- NHS Group Accounting Manual (DHSC, updated annually) — IFRS-based with adaptations.
- IFRS 16 HMT adaptation — discount rate: HMT-prescribed real / nominal rates; short-term exemption applied broadly.
- PFI / PPP — on-balance-sheet under IFRS 16 adaptation.
- Going concern — anticipated operational continuation (not financial solvency in private sense) per HM Treasury guidance.
- Annual governance statement — key controls + risks.

## Central government FReM

- Government Financial Reporting Manual — HMT annual; IFRS with adaptations.
- AME / DEL / CDEL / RDEL budget classification — critical for Supply Estimates + Estimates Memorandum.
- Consolidated Fund + Contingencies Fund accounting.
- Machinery-of-government transfers.
- Whole of Government Accounts (WGA) — Treasury consolidation.

## Higher Education SORP

- HE / FE SORP (FRS 102) specific to HE + FE bodies.
- Access + participation (OfS), research income (UKRI), fee income (regulated / unregulated).
- Endowment + restricted reserves.
- USS + TPS + LGPS — multi-employer complexity; IAS 19 / FRS 102 s28 as multi-employer where schemes unable to allocate.
- TRAC (Transparent Approach to Costing) — separate regulatory return.

## Registered social landlords (Housing SORP)

- Housing SORP 2018.
- Social housing assets — component depreciation, major-works capitalisation.
- Government grants — SHG (Social Housing Grant) amortised per SORP.
- Value for Money standard — RSH metrics + assurance.
- Rent Standard + Consumer Standards (RSH).

## Public-sector audit specifics

### Code of Audit Practice (NAO + PSAA)

Three-part report:
1. Opinion on financial statements.
2. **Regularity** — for govt / academies / other public bodies — funds used for intended purposes.
3. **Value-for-Money conclusion** — 3 criteria (financial sustainability, governance, improving economy / efficiency / effectiveness). "Qualified exception" or "no exception" narrative.

Reporting to National Audit Office / PSAA / Senior Responsible Auditor.

### Local authority going concern

Statutory presumption; audit guidance confirms going concern not a function of financial resilience alone but ability to continue providing services (HMT FReM equivalent).

## Regulatory + ethical focus

- Charities: Ethics Statement + SORP 2024 update (pending) + Charity Commission CC3 trustees' duties.
- Academies: Accounting Officer regularity statement + DFE Reviews of Academy Financial Management (RAFM).
- LAs: Section 114 notice if projected spending exceeds resources; PFI off-balance-sheet re-examination.
- NHS: emergency funding + CDEL vs RDEL classification integrity.

## Sector audit focus

- Fund accounting integrity (restricted vs unrestricted).
- Related-party disclosure completeness (lower thresholds).
- Grant income recognition — conditions + restrictions interpretation.
- Going concern under public-sector logic.
- Pension schemes — LGPS triennial valuation + IAS 19 / FRS 102 s28 disclosures + asset ceiling nuance.
- Regularity — review of funding-agreement terms + sample test of key spend categories.
- VFM — walk all three criteria; document evidence; high-impact exceptions escalated to significant weaknesses.
- Academies: trustee transactions + close-family disclosure + salary > £100k bands.
- LA: capital financing requirement + MRP + HRA ring-fence + Prudential Code.
- NHS: counting + coding in SLA (Service Level Agreement) income; working-capital loans; capital regime.

## Anti-patterns

- Using private-sector going-concern template for public body (ignoring HMT / DHSC / CIPFA frameworks).
- Charity trustees' report boilerplate without public-benefit analysis.
- Academy regularity audit treated as add-on — failure to reference funding agreement clauses.
- LA VFM conclusion given as binary pass/fail instead of narrative assessment against three criteria.
- Heritage + community asset valuation skipped "immaterial" without documented basis.
- Grant income recognised when cash received regardless of conditions (contrary to FRS 102 + SORP).
- NHS going-concern conclusion conflated with solvency analysis (wrong lens).
- MAT academy consolidated accounts with trustee disclosures only at group level (missing separate-entity details).
